SEBI Circular on Categorization and Rationalization of Mutual Fund Schemes (October 6, 2017):

SEBI issued this circular to bring uniformity in the characteristics of similar type of schemes launched by different mutual funds. Key provisions:

Equity Scheme Categories and Definitions:
1. Large Cap Fund: Minimum 80% in large cap stocks (top 100 by full market capitalisation). Benchmark: NIFTY 100 or similar.
2. Large & Mid Cap Fund: Minimum 35% each in large cap and mid cap stocks.
3. Mid Cap Fund: Minimum 65% in mid cap stocks (101st to 250th by market capitalisation). Benchmark: Nifty Midcap 150 or similar.
4. Small Cap Fund: Minimum 65% in small cap stocks (251st and beyond).
5. Multi Cap Fund: Minimum 75% in equity with at least 25% each in large, mid, small cap.
6. Flexi Cap Fund: Minimum 65% in equity, flexible allocation across caps. Benchmark: NIFTY 500 or similar.
7. ELSS: Minimum 80% in equity, mandatory 3-year lock-in, tax benefit under Section 80C.
8. Sectoral/Thematic: Minimum 80% in stocks of a specific sector or theme.
9. Focused Fund: Maximum 30 stocks, minimum 65% in equity.
10. Value/Contra: Value or contrarian investment strategy with minimum 65% equity.

Riskometer Requirements:
All mutual fund schemes must prominently display a riskometer in all scheme-related communications. The riskometer has six levels: Low, Low to Moderate, Moderate, Moderately High, High, Very High. AMCs must evaluate and update the riskometer on a monthly basis.

Benchmark Index Requirements:
Every scheme must declare a benchmark index for performance comparison. Total Return Index (TRI) versions must be used instead of Price Return Index for more accurate benchmarking.

HDFC Fund Benchmarks per SEBI:
- HDFC Top 100 / Large Cap Fund: NIFTY 100 TRI
- HDFC Flexi Cap Fund: NIFTY 500 TRI
- HDFC ELSS Tax Saver Fund: NIFTY 500 TRI
- HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund: Nifty Midcap 150 TRI
